2012 – now

SoundCloud, Berlin

I initially joined SoundCloud's Discovery team, and was the lead architect and engineer for search. I migrated from Solr to Elasticsearch, rebuilt the indexing and query pipelines, and integrated machine learning models for ranking and relevance. Heavy emphasis was placed on correctness, operational (dev-ops) simplicity, and scalability. The resulting infrastructure continues to power the search product today.

I moved to the Activities team, and rebuilt the data system that powers several time-series event streams. The core of that system is Roshi, an open-source, CRDT-based distributed system for timestamped events. The SoundCloud stream, and several other major product features, are powered by Roshi.

Currently, I'm on the Infrastructure team, working on our next-generation application platform.

2010 – 2012

IPTEGO, Berlin

IPTEGO was a small company that provided a flagship network and telephony monitoring product called PALLADION. I initially contributed several protocol parsers and analyzers for PALLADION, including RTCP, MEGACO, DIAMETER, and RTP. In time I moved to the core technology team, and helped scale the product to handle much larger (continent-sized) installations. Eventually, I became a product manager for that team, and (among other things) became responsible for build and release management.

When I left, IPTEGO was acquired by Acme Packet. Around a year later, Acme Packet was acquired by Oracle.

2009 – 2010

Sabbatical, Johannesburg

I spent a year in Johannesburg, South Africa, volunteering for several NGOs, and contributing to some open-source and personal projects.

2006 – 2009

Bloomberg, New York City

Bloomberg is an information services company, primarily focusing on financial data. I joined Bloomberg's BLAW team, which provided a search engine and research tools for corporate lawyers. I was primarily responsible for BBLS, a federated, proprietary legal document search engine. In my time there, I developed the infrastructure to allow it to grow from dozens to thousands of concurrent users, and from tens to hundreds of millions of documents. That involved defining and maintaining strict SLAs, system health monitoring, and code quality control measures.

2002 – 2006

ADTRAN, Huntsville

ADTRAN manufactures network equipment for carriers and the enterprise. I was a co-op student engineer (similar to an ongoing internship) in several different departments. I implemented SNMP and TFTP features on embedded-class hardware, and developed multiple new features for ADTRAN's enterprise routers and switches, with an emphasis on security and VPN technologies.

2001 – 2006

Clemson University, Clemson

I studied and earned a Bachelor of Science (B.S.) in Computer Engineering, which is a mix of pure Computer Science and Electrical Engineering, with an emphasis on software engineering and network (distributed) programming.
